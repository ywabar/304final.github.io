Introduction

For the purpose of this assignment our group collectively decided to choose generative artificial intelligence also known as generative AI as our technology of choice to investigate its ethical and social effect. Specifically focusing on generative artificial intelligence in the realm of videos and graphics.

According to Feuerriegel et al. (2023), “The term generative AI refers to computational techniques that are capable of generating seemingly new, meaningful content such as text, images, or audio from training data” (Feuerriegel et al., 2023, section 2, para. 2). Additionally, Generative AI is based on a machine learning architecture where the technology is able to generate meaningful content using raw data and constructing learning patterns, upon user inputs to output significant content. The outputs which are produced can only be initiated if the machine learning architecture has a preset of raw data which can be used to create a variation of the original raw data stored on the machine learning system (IBM, 2023). Without a preset of raw data into the machine learning algorithm generative AI would not exist.

Generative AI has gained a lot of popularity and controversy over the past few years, for good reason. It allows anybody to utilize this technology to generate any sort of text given a topic with minimal response time, producing creative images or videos, all which can be viewed as legitimate, meaning nobody would recognize that a specific product or content was produced by an artificial intelligence system. Given this, it is clear that such a powerful tool will have its positive and negative effects determined by the intention of a user. That is why It is crucial to understand how this technology affects our society and investigate the ethical concerns that may arise using generative AI which is the subject of this investigation.

Applications of The Technology

One example is In the healthcare industry, Generative AI is changing the way we approach illness diagnosis and treatment planning. For example, AI systems may examine medical pictures such as X-rays or MRIs to discover anomalies, assisting in early illness identification. Another use of Generative AI is in the field of content creation, where it is used to write articles, create art, and even compose music. This technology can help in the creation of creative material, thereby increasing productivity and creativity.

Despite its promise, the complexity of medical data and the importance of health-related choices face substantial obstacles. Accuracy is critical since AI misinterpretations might lead to inaccurate diagnoses. Furthermore, because patient data is very sensitive, integrating AI into the healthcare system requires addressing privacy concerns. When it comes to generative AI in content production, the main issue is one of ethics and intellectual property. There is a danger that AI-generated material will infringe on current copyrights or produce content that is indistinguishable from human-created works. This calls into question the concept of uniqueness as well as the depreciation of human creativity. Furthermore, given the AI's reliance on the data it is trained on, ensuring that the AI-generated material is devoid of biases and mistakes is a substantial challenge. The use of Generative AI matters because it has the potential to transform a wide range of industries by enhancing efficiency, accuracy, and creativity. Early diagnosis in healthcare can save lives. It has the potential to democratize creativity and make content generating accessible to a larger audience in content development.

Critical Discussion and Applications

The way we are able to look at Generative AI in different ethical lenses is by considering different perspectives when considering its uses and impact. Such Lenses can include Utilitarian Lens which we would consider whether Generative AI has an overall societal benefit or harm. We can consider whether or not it provides a positive impact on our daily productivity and efficiency leading to an increase or decrease of our social growth. Another lens we can look at is the Virtue based lenses. With this lens we consider whether the use of Generative AI can promote and highlight virtues such as honesty and integrity. We would look at the development of such AI and if we can cultivate such qualities within AI to ensure its development and usage is within positive societal values. The way we look at Generative AI within different ethical lenses is mainly based on perspective and the values we keep when considering its value and as such the societal impacts vary. Within different lenses we must consider how Generative AI can hit and miss certain expectations and we must be open minded in capturing it all.

There are many recommendations we can suggest to uphold the integrity of Generative AI and to mitigate the potential harm of its use. We must ensure transparency between AI and the users. There are barriers between our understanding of AI and the process of how AI makes decisions. By investing more time into improved communication between AI and users we can break down this barrier to improve transparency and trust between both groups. Another method we can employ is the use of educating users with the capabilities and limitations of Generative AI. By doing so we can ensure users are aware of the potential and usability of the technology and to mitigate exceeding expectations. Another area we can consider is eliminating or mitigating the bias within AI algorithms. Generative AI can demonstrate non-biased responses can help promote fairness and be more open with the usage in different areas. In terms of policy related recommendations, we can consider implementing Ethical Design Practices. Implementing such practices can help designers with guidelines that will help them consider societal impacts and potential consequences with the development of such technology.
